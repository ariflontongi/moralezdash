Can Tourist Wear Kandura In Dubai

Posted by Billy Koelling on Monday, August 5, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Kandura in Dubai: Rocking the White Thobe as a Tourist - Yes, You Can (But Maybe You Shouldn't...Unless?)

Ever looked at those flowing white robes Emirati men wear and thought, "Man, that looks comfy in that desert heat!" Well, my friend, those robes are called kanduras (also sometimes called dishdashas), and the answer to your question is yes, technically, you can wear one as a tourist in Dubai.

However, before you ditch your floral shirt and board shorts (hold that thought, board shorts are a definite no-no), there are a few things to consider.

Respect the Culture, Avoid the Costume Party Vibe

Dubai is a cosmopolitan city, but it's also a place with deep cultural roots. While there's no fashion police forcing tourists into abayas (the traditional dress for women), wearing a kandura as a novelty outfit might not be the best look. Think of it this way: would you wear a lederhosen to Buckingham Palace just because it's fancy legwear? Probably not.

But hey, if you're genuinely interested in Emirati culture and want to experience it firsthand, then go for it! Just remember to be respectful.

Mastering the Kandura: A Crash Course (Kind Of)

So, you're decided. Kandura time! Here's a lightning-fast guide:

  • Finding your fit: Kanduras come in various sizes. Ideally, you'll want one that's not too baggy or too tight.
  • Accessorize with care: A plain white kandura is perfectly fine, but you can add a shemag (headscarf) and some sandals for a more complete look. Just avoid anything too flashy or gaudy.
  • The confidence factor: Rocking a kandura is all about owning it. Walk tall, shoulders back, and project that "I meant to wear this" kind of vibe.

Remember, a little effort goes a long way!

When is a Kandura a Good Idea?

Here are a few situations where your kandura might be a welcome addition:

  • Cultural events: Attending a local festival or art exhibition? Throwing on a kandura shows respect for the occasion.
  • Visiting religious sites: Mosques often have dress code restrictions. A kandura ensures you're appropriately covered.
  • Making friends with the locals: Emiratis appreciate visitors who take an interest in their culture. A kandura might just be the conversation starter you need!

When to Maybe Reconsider the Kandura

  • Beach days: Let's be honest, kanduras aren't exactly beachwear.
  • Fine dining: Fancy restaurants might have a dress code that a kandura wouldn't fit.
  • Feeling awkward: If you're not comfortable or feel like you're sticking out in a bad way, ditch the kandura. Confidence is key!

Kandura FAQs for the Curious Tourist

How to wear a shemag? There are many ways to tie a shemag. A quick online search will show you various styles.

How to wash a kandura? Follow the care instructions on the label. Generally, a gentle wash with cold water is recommended.

How much does a kandura cost? Prices vary depending on quality and material. You can find them for a few hundred dirhams at textile shops.

How to act when wearing a kandura? Be respectful of local customs. Avoid loud noises or inappropriate behavior.

How to avoid looking like a tourist in a costume? Pair your kandura with genuine curiosity and respect for the culture.

So there you have it! The complete (well, mostly complete) guide to rocking a kandura in Dubai. Remember, it's all about being respectful, comfortable, and maybe a little bit adventurous. Now go forth and experience Dubai in style (or at least, comfortable coolness)!
