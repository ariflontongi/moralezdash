Who is Colette McEachin? Meet Donald McEachin's Wife

Posted by Tandra Barner on Thursday, August 29, 2024

Colette McEachin (born in b/w 1960, 1961, age: 60-61 years) is a Richmond City Commonwealth’s Attorney in Virginia, America. She has been known for being the wife of American politician and lawyer Mr. Donald McEachin.

Her husband has served last as a member of the U.S. House of Representatives from Virginia’s 4th District. Her husband died on 28th November 2022 and just after her husband’s death Colette became a subject of media talks.

Colette won Richmond City Commonwealth Attorney election on November 2, 2021, and since then she is holding this position.

Colette McEachin Biography & Education

Sharing about Colette McEachin from different sources then let us tell you that she was born in Colorado Springs, Colorado in b/w 1960-1961. She then grew up with her family in Connecticut. Her parents are having a background in Army. She lived as a smart kid of her parents and completed her graduation from Brown University.

After completing her degree from Brown University she began working and after a few years, she realized to get a degree in Law. She then got graduated from the University of Virginia School of Law.

Career- Political Personality, Attorney

Getting her J.D. from the Virginia School of Law Colette McEachin began working for Civil Law firms in Atlanta and in Richmond. She then joined Richmond Commonwealths Attorney’s Office.

She is now a member of the Richmond community and the Commonwealth’s Attorney’s Office for more than 25 years. She remained privileged in serving in the Criminal Law Section of the Virginia State Bar, the Board of Visitors for Virginia Commonwealth University, the Hill Tucker Bar Association, and other positions.

According to her Ballotpedia profile, she got selected in the general election for Richmond City Commonwealth’s Attorney first time in 2019 with a vote of 55968. And again she won the election in 2021. She represents Democratic Party. She holds the title of being the first woman ever selected as Richmond Commonwealth Attorney.
