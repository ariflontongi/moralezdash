Can You Gift A Car Twice In Washington State

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, August 4, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Gifting a Car in Washington: Sharing the Wheels, Not the Tax Bill (Twice!)

Ah, the joy of gifting a car! It's like passing on a mechanical steed, a trusty chariot (with slightly less dramatic entrances, hopefully). But in Washington state, there's a wrinkle in the gifting road – a tax twist you might want to navigate. Buckle up, for we're diving into the world of car gifting and the question on everyone's mind: Can you gift a car twice in Washington and still score that sweet tax exemption?

The Gift that Keeps on Giving (Except for the Taxman)

The good news is, gifting a car in Washington can be a tax-friendly affair – as long as it's the first time the car is being titled in the state. Washington offers an exemption on sales tax if the donor (the generous you!) has already paid Washington sales tax on the vehicle. This means you can surprise your lucky recipient with a car and they won't owe a hefty tax bill on top of it (cue celebratory car honking!).

But here's the catch: This tax exemption doesn't apply to cars that have been gifted multiple times in a row. Think of it like a one-time-use coupon – use it wisely! If the car has already been gifted once within Washington, then a sales/use tax will likely be due when it's transferred again.

So You Want to Play Gift-o-Rama with a Car? Here's the Plan

Planning on gifting a car that's already been a Washington gift? No worries, there are still options!

  • Be honest! On the title transfer paperwork, clearly state the car's being gifted. This helps the Department of Licensing determine the appropriate taxes.
  • The recipient might owe some dough. They'll likely be responsible for paying sales/use tax based on the car's current value.

Remember, it's always best to check with the Washington Department of Licensing for the most up-to-date information on titling and taxes. They're the car-gifting gurus!

Frequently Asked Gifting Questions (FAQ):

How to transfer a car title in Washington?

The Washington Department of Licensing (https://dol.wa.gov/vehicles-and-boats/vehicle-registration/transfer-title-buying-private-party) has all the info you need!

How much does it cost to transfer a car title in Washington?

Fees vary depending on the vehicle, but you can find the details on the Department of Licensing website.

How long do I have to transfer a car title in Washington?

You have 15 days to transfer ownership after receiving the car as a gift. Don't delay, or you might face penalties!

How do I prove I paid Washington sales tax on the car?

The Department of Licensing might ask for proof, like your original sales tax receipt.

Can I gift a car out of state?

Yes, but the titling and tax rules will depend on the recipient's state.

So there you have it! Gifting a car in Washington can be a smooth ride, just be mindful of the tax implications. Now go forth and spread vehicular joy (responsibly, of course)!
