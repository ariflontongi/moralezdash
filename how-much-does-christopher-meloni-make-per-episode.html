Christopher Meloni’s Early Career

Before we discuss Meloni’s earnings per episode, it’s important to understand the trajectory of his career. Christopher Meloni began his acting career in the late 1980s with small roles in television and films. His breakout role came in 1999 when he was cast as Detective Elliot Stabler in “Law & Order: SVU.” This role would define his career and establish him as a household name.

Rise to Fame with “Law & Order: SVU”

Meloni’s portrayal of the tough yet compassionate detective earned him critical acclaim and a loyal fan base. He starred in “SVU” for 12 seasons before leaving the show in 2011. During his tenure, he became one of the highest-paid actors on television.

Christopher Meloni’s Salary Evolution

Meloni’s salary on “SVU” saw a significant increase over the years. In the early seasons, his earnings per episode were modest compared to his later seasons. By the time he left the show in 2011, reports suggested that he was making upwards of $395,000 per episode.
