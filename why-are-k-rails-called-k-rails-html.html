It’s called a “Jersey Curb” because it was invented in New Jersey along the Turnpike in the late 1950s and early 1960s. Surprisingly, it was based on an innovative concrete divider installed on Grapevine Grade in the middle of US 99 by the California Division of Highways.

Also, why is it called a Jersey barrier? 

The term “Jersey barrier” refers to concrete barriers that were first used by the New Jersey State Highway Department to divide multiple highway lanes in the 1950s. Between 1955 and 1959, Stevens Institute of Technology created the barrier design that was first used in New Jersey.
