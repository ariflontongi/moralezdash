Early Life and Career Beginnings

Ben Savage was born Bennett Joseph Savage in Chicago, Illinois. He began his acting career at a young age, making his debut in his elder brother Fred Savage’s film “Little Monsters.” His early career also included roles in movies like “Big Girls Don’t Cry… They Get Even” and “Clifford.”

Rise to Stardom

Savage’s breakthrough came when he was cast as Cory Matthews in the ABC sitcom “Boy Meets World.” The show, which ran from 1993 to 2000, was a massive success and catapulted Savage to stardom. His portrayal of Cory Matthews earned him critical acclaim and a Young Artist Award.

Continued Success in Television

After “Boy Meets World,” Savage continued to find success in television. He reprised his role as Cory Matthews in the Disney Channel spin-off series “Girl Meets World,” which aired from 2014 to 2017. He has also made guest appearances in various TV shows like “Still Standing,” “Without a Trace,” and “Bones.”

Transition into Directing

In addition to acting, Savage has also ventured into directing. He directed several episodes of “Girl Meets World,” showcasing his versatility and expanding his sources of income.

Endorsements and Other Ventures

Savage’s wealth is not solely derived from his acting and directing career. He has also earned money from endorsements and brand partnerships. He has been associated with brands like Honda and Dunkin’ Donuts, which have contributed to his net worth.

Real Estate Investments

Philanthropy
