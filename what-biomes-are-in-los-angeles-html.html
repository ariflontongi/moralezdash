What Biomes Are In Los Angeles California And Washington Dc

Posted by Billy Koelling on Tuesday, August 13, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

So You Wanna Be a Biome Buster? Unveiling the Wild Sides of LA and DC

Ever wondered if your city is more suited for sun-baked lizards or squirrel-hoarding chipmunks? Fear not, fellow citizen-scientist (or maybe just someone stuck in a particularly dull meeting), because today we're diving into the fascinating world of biomes! But not just any biomes, oh no. We're going on a coast-to-coast adventure to uncover the secret ecosystems hiding in plain sight, in the bustling metropolises of Los Angeles and Washington D.C.

LA La Land: Not Just for Beach Bums (Though There Are Plenty of Those)

Los Angeles, the land of dreams and questionable spray tans, might conjure up images of Hollywood glitz and endless sunshine. But beneath the veneer of red carpets and palm trees lies a surprising truth: Los Angeles is a Mediterranean biome! Yes, you read that right, folks. Think sun-drenched summers, mild, wet winters, and a landscape that could have been plucked straight out of Tuscany (minus the vineyards, maybe). This translates to rolling hills dotted with chaparral shrubs – those tough, drought-resistant plants that thrive in the hot, dry season.

Now, don't get too excited about spotting lions and tigers roaming the Hollywood Hills (although that would be one heck of a movie). The animal kingdom here is a little less "Jurassic Park" and a little more "coyote serenading you at 3 am." But hey, there's a certain charm to that, right?

D.C. Drama: More Than Just Political Gridlock

Washington D.C., the heart of American politics, might seem like a concrete jungle at first glance. But beneath the towering monuments and power suits lies a different kind of jungle – a temperate deciduous forest to be precise. Imagine a scene straight out of a history textbook: towering oak trees changing colors in fall, squirrels gathering nuts for the winter, and the gentle pitter-patter of rain on leaves. Sounds pretty peaceful, right? Well, that is, until election season rolls around, and things get a little more…heated (pun intended).

Now, D.C.'s wildlife might not be as flamboyant as LA's coyotes, but there's still plenty to see. Keep your eyes peeled for deer wandering through Rock Creek Park, or the occasional red fox slinking through an alleyway. Just remember, these creatures are used to dodging politicians, so they're probably not fazed by you taking their picture (unlike some of those senators).

The Verdict: Biome Bragging Rights

So, who wins the biome battle, LA or D.C.? Honestly, it's a tie! LA gets points for its unique Mediterranean flair, while D.C. scores high for its classic, fall-foliage vibes. The real winner, of course, is you – the newly minted biome buff who can now impress your friends at parties with your newfound knowledge of shrublands and deciduous delights.

Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a sudden urge to buy a pair of khakis and binoculars. Maybe there's a coyote convention happening in the Hollywood Hills that I need to attend…
