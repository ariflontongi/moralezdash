Motoo Abiko’s Arts Career, Manga – Fujiko Fujio Member

Abiko was an avid drawing lover and his passion for drawing increased when he met Hiroshi Fujimoto in his elementary school. Both became friends because of their love for creating cartoons and stories. In high school, they decided to become manga artists and create cartoons and series. Initially, they created stories and send them to periodicals.

They formed their partnership named ‘Fujiko Fujio’ in 1951. They created a lot of series together including Doraemon, Little Ghost Q-Taro, and others. As an individual, Motoo Abiko created Ninja Hattori, Kurobē, Futa-kun, Billy Dog, Love Thief, Manga Roads, and many more series. Hiroshi Fujimoto and Motoo broke their partnership in 1987.
